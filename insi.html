Research

Problem

How might we help UCSD students quickly find locations that meet their personal study space preferences in order to maximize their time to attend to other responsibilities as students?

Mission

We aim to provide resources about study spaces so that the overall process of finding suitable spots can be done more quickly and be easier for UCSD students.

Design/Development

Moodboard

We wanted our app to have an overall simple and clean feel for most efficient use while still being lively and welcoming for users which can be seen in our moodboards.

Style Guide

We designed components that could be used throughout the project and standardized interactions and layouts to ensure consistency in our product.　 For the color and components of our app, we chose Helvetica, an ADA-compliant typeface, for its sleek look and chose 2 soft colors in addition to white and black, which form an ADA-compliant color palette.

Conclusion

Takeaways

Creating components for the digital prototypes should be saved for once you have reached your hi-fidelity stage. In the early stage, we were quickly throwing out ideas and it was an inefficient use of time to make each idea into a reusable component when we still didn’t know if it would make it into the final stage. Sometimes over-engineering is counterproductive.
